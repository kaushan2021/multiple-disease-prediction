Dictionary

  • Age: The age of the individual, typically measured in years.
  • Sex: The biological sex of the individual, often categorized as male or female.
  • CP (Chest Pain): This refers to the type of chest pain experienced by the individual, often categorized into different levels of severity.
  • Trestbps (Resting Blood Pressure): The individual's blood pressure measured at rest, typically in millimeters of mercury (mmHg).
  • Chol (Cholesterol): The level of cholesterol in the individual's blood, usually measured in milligrams per deciliter (mg/dL).
  • Fbs (Fasting Blood Sugar): The level of glucose (sugar) in the individual's blood after fasting, often measured in milligrams per deciliter (mg/dL).
  • Restecg (Resting Electrocardiographic Results): The results of an electrocardiogram (ECG/EKG) taken while the individual is at rest, which measures the electrical activity of the heart.
  • Thalach (Maximum Heart Rate Achieved): The highest heart rate achieved during exercise or stress testing.
  • Exang (Exercise-Induced Angina): A binary indicator (often 1 for yes, 0 for no) indicating whether the individual experiences angina (chest pain) during exercise.
  • Oldpeak (ST Depression Induced by Exercise Relative to Rest): The amount of depression in the ST segment of the ECG during exercise compared to rest, which can indicate myocardial ischemia.
  • Slope: The slope of the ST segment during exercise, which can provide further information about the presence and severity of coronary artery disease.
  • CA (Number of Major Vessels Colored by Fluoroscopy): The number of major blood vessels (coronary arteries) colored by a dye during a fluoroscopy procedure, indicating potential blockages or stenosis.
  • Thal (Thalassemia): A type of blood disorder that can affect oxygen transport in the body, often categorized into different types or severities.
