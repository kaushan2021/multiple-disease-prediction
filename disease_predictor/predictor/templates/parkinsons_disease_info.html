Parkinson's Disease Information

Parkinson's disease is a neurodegenerative disorder that affects movement control. It develops gradually, and its symptoms may include tremors, stiffness, and difficulty with balance and coordination.

The primary cause is the loss of dopamine-producing cells in the brain. While there is no cure for Parkinson's disease, various treatments can help manage symptoms and improve quality of life.

Early diagnosis, medication, physical therapy, and lifestyle modifications are key components of Parkinson's disease management.
